SECTION 01 // OPERATIVE BRIEFING
THE COLLAPSE // RESTRICTED SUMMARY

World War III did not end with a treaty. It ended with a silence. The convergence of conventional warfare, a nuclear detonation over the Black Sea, engineered pandemic, and complete societal breakdown claimed over three billion lives. What the war did not kill, the aftermath threatened to finish.

Analysts now refer to the period following the Collapse as SPIKE — a global wave of psychological breakdown, violent crime, and societal collapse driven by what clinicians termed "Emotional Sickness." Civilization was three decisions from extinction.

THE RESET // YEAR 0

The restructured United Nations — now operating as the United Global Directorate (UGD) — commissioned four global research programs to address the crisis. Of these, the neurological research conducted at a facility in the former United States proved most effective.

At the beginning of Year 0, a compassionate neurological intervention known as the Reset was implemented worldwide. The Reset stabilized the global population's neurological baseline. SPIKE ended. Rebuilding began. The world has been at peace for five years.

YEAR 5 // THE WORLD TODAY

Five years have passed. The UGD administers a stable global society from its central chambers. National borders as they existed before the Collapse have dissolved in favor of regional administrative districts under Directorate oversight. A functional global communications network connects communities. Food production, infrastructure, and basic services have been restored across most of the inhabited world.

The world is orderly. It is functional. By every measurable standard, it is at peace. You serve that peace.

SPIKE // THE RETURNING SICKNESS

SPIKE is the UGD's clinical designation for deviation above neurological baseline:

Structural cognition collapse
Perception decay
Inhibition loss
Kinetic misfiring
Emotional corrosion

The Reset stabilized all surviving citizens. SPIKE represents the return or increase of emotional dysregulation above that stable baseline.

SPIKE is measured on a scale of 0 to 11 via calibrated EEG wand or implant — a diagnostic standard developed by the pre-Reset UN and retained post-Reset as essential field equipment. A reading of 0 is normal. Elevated readings indicate increasing neurological instability and must be managed.

SPIKE is not weakness. It is a medical condition. Operatives experiencing elevated readings are expected to report them, take prescribed countermeasures, and continue the mission.

⚠ FIELD HAZARD // SPIKED INDIVIDUALS

Field operatives may encounter individuals registering extreme SPIKE levels. These individuals are typically in acute neurological distress — non-communicative, erratic, and potentially dangerous. Their condition is distressing to observe.

Operatives are instructed to limit interaction with SPIKED individuals where possible. Extended proximity has been observed to elevate operative SPIKE levels. Do not attempt intervention without Medical certification. Document all encounters and report to UGD Medical Services upon return.

⚠ INCIDENT REPORT // THE SPIKE STORM

Shortly before this mission's commissioning, a SPIKE Storm — a widespread, simultaneous SPIKE elevation event — was recorded across a broad geographic region. Affected individuals experienced rapid, involuntary escalation of SPIKE readings. Isolated cases reached critical levels. Casualties have been confirmed. Origin and mechanism remain under active investigation.

This anomaly is among the primary factors prompting the Directorate's renewed interest in RE-01. Operatives should anticipate elevated ambient SPIKE risk in the field. Maintain your baseline.

YOUR MISSION // OPERATION: REEVES TRIALS

You are UGD Operatives, commissioned by the Directorate's Division IV to investigate Research Establishment 01 (RE-01), located in what was formerly the state of Indiana, United States. RE-01 was identified in recovered UGD archives as a site of early SPIKE-related neurological research — the nature and outcomes of which are, at present, classified or unknown.

Your objectives are as follows:

  • Primary: Locate and retrieve any surviving research documentation from RE-01.
  • Primary: Assess the current state and any remaining security infrastructure of the facility.
  • Secondary: Identify any links between RE-01's research and other known global facilities.
  • Secondary: Document any anomalous phenomena or readings encountered on-site.

Intelligence on RE-01 is limited. Conditions inside are unknown. Prioritize extraction over engagement. Keep your SPIKE readings low.

EMOTIONAL FLAWS

Emotional Flaws are assigned when a character suffers extreme trauma. They are roleplay-first — they define how your operative is shaped by what they've seen. Lean into them for small mechanical rewards, or resist them at a cost. Flaws resolve only through significant narrative action. The flaws listed below are examples — the GM and player may define others appropriate to the operative's history.

LEAN INTO IT
Act according to the flaw's impulse.
Gain +1 to the roll OR +1 COMPOSURE.
RESIST IT
Roll d10 + RESOLVE vs TN 11.
Success: act normally. Failure: -1 to roll OR +1 SPIKE (GM's choice).
Fear of Attachment
Trigger: Forming or relying on close bonds. Effect: -1 to social rolls involving trust. Lean Bonus: +1 when acting independently.
Bottled Rage
Trigger: Frustration, disrespect, loss of control. Effect: Risk of aggressive response. Lean Bonus: +1 to Close Combat.
Emotional Numbness
Trigger: High-stress or emotional situations. Effect: -1 to social connection rolls. Lean Bonus: Immune to first COMPOSURE loss per scene.
Paranoia
Trigger: Uncertainty, hidden information. Effect: Difficulty trusting others. Lean Bonus: +1 to Awareness checks.
Survivor's Guilt
Trigger: Others being harmed or at risk. Effect: Hesitation or self-sacrifice pressure. Lean Bonus: +1 when actively protecting others.
Control Fixation
Trigger: Chaotic or unpredictable situations. Effect: Must attempt to control outcomes. Lean Bonus: +1 to planning or preparation actions.

SECTION 05 // THE SPIKE SCALE
WHAT IS SPIKE?

SPIKE is your operative's measure of neurological deviation from Reset baseline. It runs from 0 to 11. SPIKE increases in response to trauma, extreme emotional stimuli, certain hostile environments, and specific threats encountered in the field. It decreases through rest and deliberate grounding techniques.

SPIKE is not failure. It is pressure. Every operative will feel it. The measure of a field operative is not whether their SPIKE rises — it is whether they manage it before it manages them.

LEVELDESIGNATIONMECHANICAL EFFECT
0 SERENE +1 to logic and TECH rolls. Cannot critically fail SPIKE Saves.
1–2 STABLE No mechanical effect. Nominal baseline.
3–5 SHAKEN -1 to all MIND rolls. Eligible for SPIKE Saves (d20 + RESOLVE vs TN 9) when triggered.
6–7 CRACKED -1 to MIND and RESOLVE. Hallucinations and memory distortion become possible — Saving Throw required when triggered.
8–9 OVERLOADED Disadvantage on all social checks. Operative becomes unreasonable and non-compliant in roleplay. SPIKE Save required hourly.
10 SPIKED Loss of character control. Roll on the Behavior Table (d20) at the start of each turn. At end of turn: d20 + RESOLVE vs TN 17. Natural 20: SPIKE -2. Natural 1: SPIKE +1.
11 CONSUMED Take 1d6 True Damage per turn until HP reaches 0. Then take 1d4 RESOLVE damage per turn until 0. Death follows. End of turn: d20 + RESOLVE vs TN 19. Natural 1: damage rolls at Advantage.
LOWERING SPIKE
  • Short Rest: SPIKE -1
  • Long Rest: SPIKE -2
  • Grounding Action: Once per encounter, take an Action and roll d20 + MIND vs TN 13. On success, SPIKE -1. An ally with SPIKE 4 or lower may grant Advantage on this roll.
Assisting a SPIKED Operative

A teammate may attempt to ground a SPIKED operative using logic, routine, or calm command. Requires an Action and roleplay.

d20 + MIND vs TN 14
  • Success: Target gains Advantage on their next SPIKE Save
  • Failure: The SPIKED operative lashes out — 1d4 damage to helper
  • Critical Failure: Both make a RESOLVE Save (TN 12). Target has Disadvantage on their next SPIKE Save.

SPIKE RESONANCE

When two individuals' SPIKE levels differ by 4 or more, the individual with the lower level experiences a discomforting instability — described as "static under the skin" — in proximity to the higher-SPIKE individual. Both suffer Disadvantage on social checks while the gap persists.

Report resonance effects in character where possible. It is a documented field phenomenon and should be logged upon mission return.

SECTION 06 // ADVANCEMENT
MILESTONE PROGRESSION

Advancement is milestone-based. The GM will signal when the party reaches a milestone. At each milestone, each operative may choose one of the following improvements:

Gain a New Skill
Add any untrained skill at Trained level (+2).
Specialize a Skill
Upgrade a Trained skill (+2) to Specialized (+3).
Gain a Trait
Select any trait from Section 02. Maximum of 6 traits per operative.
Increase Maximum COMPOSURE
+1 to maximum COMPOSURE. Subject to GM-set campaign cap.
Reduce an Emotional Flaw
Rare. Requires significant narrative resolution across multiple sessions and GM approval.
PROGRESSION BEYOND STATISTICS

Mechanical advancement is one dimension of growth. Operatives also progress through the world itself:

  • Knowledge: What you discover stays with you. Information is a resource — sometimes the only one that matters.
  • Equipment: Better tools may be recovered from the field or requisitioned from the Directorate.
  • Access: Recovered credentials, clearance codes, and bypassed security open doors that force cannot.
  • Allies: Contacts who owe your team something may be worth more than any weapon you carry.
  • Information Networks: Knowing who to ask — and who to trust — changes the shape of every mission that follows.
