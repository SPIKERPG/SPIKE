GM CALIBRATION NOTES

When in doubt, use TN 9. Raise it when environment, opposition, or consequence warrants — not because the moment feels important. Importance is a story tool, not a TN modifier.

Advantage = roll 2 dice, take higher. Disadvantage = roll 2 dice, take lower. Nat 10 (d10) = +2 damage OR narrative bonus. Nat 1 = complication, not automatic failure.

SPIKE-related rolls (saves, recovery, assistance) always use the d20. All controlled actions use the d10.

02 // SPIKE SYSTEM
0
SERENE
Baseline. No mechanical effect.
1–2
STABLE
Functioning. No penalties. Roleplay opportunity only.
3–5
SHAKEN
–1 to MIND checks. Cracks beginning to show. Memory flashbacks are possible as GM tool.
6–7
CRACKED
–1 MIND and –1 RESOLVE. Hallucinations possible — prompt Saving Throw. SPIKE Resonance triggers if difference with another PC is ≥4.
8–9
OVERLOADED
Disadvantage on all social rolls. Player must roleplay as unreasonable or non-compliant. Emotional Flaws are harder to resist.
10
SPIKED
Loss of character control. Roll d20 vs Behavior Table at start of turn. At end of each turn: d20+RESOLVE vs TN 17. Nat 20 = SPIKE –2. Nat 1 = SPIKE +1.
11
CONSUMED
1d6 True Damage at start of each turn until 0 HP, then 1d4 RESOLVE damage per turn. Death follows. End-of-turn save: d20+RESOLVE vs TN 19. Nat 1 = damage advantage.
LOWERING SPIKE
  • Short Rest: –1 SPIKE
  • Long Rest: –2 SPIKE
  • Action (once/encounter): d20+MIND vs TN 13. Advantage if helped by PC at SPIKE ≤4.
SPIKE RESONANCE

When two characters' SPIKE levels differ by ≥4, social scenes become unstable. Apply tension, misread cues, or prompt saves as needed.

BREAKING POINT (DR onwards)

At SPIKE 10 for the 2nd+ time: d10+RESOLVE vs TN 12. Success: AWOKEN, SPIKE drops to 4. Failure: SPIKE = 9, gain Emotional Flaw. Cannot retry until flaw is addressed.

NON-AWAKENED GROUNDING

Takes an action. Requires roleplay. Roll d20+MIND vs TN 14.

  • Success: SPIKED PC gains Advantage on next SPIKE save
  • Failure: SPIKED PC lashes out — 1d4 damage to helper
  • Critical Failure: Both make RESOLVE save vs TN 12. SPIKED PC gets Disadvantage on next save.
AWAKENED GROUNDING

Roll d20+EMPATHY vs TN 16.

  • Success: Target SPIKE –1
  • Critical Success: +1d4 to reduction; Awoken PC's EMPATHY +1
  • Failure: No effect
  • Critical Failure: Awoken PC gains +1d4 SPIKE
03 // SPIKED BEHAVIOR TABLE
USAGE

When a PC reaches SPIKE 10, they roll d20 at the start of their turn. The result determines their action. How they fulfill it is the player's roleplay. The result is tragic, not villainous.

1 — SHATTER POINT
SPIKE Save immediately
Success: act normally. Failure: +1 SPIKE (risks CONSUMED).
2 — EMOTIONAL BLEED
Instability radiates outward
All CLOSE allies: SPIKE Save vs TN 14 or +1 SPIKE. –1 to all your rolls this turn.
3 — MISPLACED TRUST
Latches onto nearest creature
Treats them as ally regardless of reality. If they harm you this turn: +1 SPIKE.
4 — VIOLENT OUTBURST
Attack nearest creature
Must use full Action. If no target in range → move toward nearest.
5 — PARANOID BREAK
Everyone is a threat
Treat all others as hostile until end of turn. Cannot benefit from Assist.
6 — PANIC RESPONSE
Must flee
Use movement to flee perceived danger. Take cover if possible. Cannot move toward enemies.
7 — RECKLESS ADVANCE
Surge forward, no regard for safety
Must move toward nearest threat. Advantage on next attack. All attacks against you have Advantage.
8 — DISASSOCIATION
Detach from reality
–2 to all rolls this turn. Cannot take Reactions.
9 — FREEZE
Immobilized by overload
Lose Action. May still take Minor Action.
10 — COLLAPSE
Body gives out
Fall prone. Lose Action. Minor Action only. Gain +1 COMPOSURE at end of turn.
11 — SENSORY OVERLOAD
Environment becomes unbearable
Disadvantage on all rolls. Must reposition away from stimuli. If unable to move: –2 to all rolls.
12 — ERRATIC ACTION
Act but roll 1d10 first
1–3: target wrong creature. 4–6: drop/mishandle item. 7–10: act normally.
13 — ECHO LOOP

14 — TARGET LOCK

16 — EMOTIONAL SURGE
+2 to attack roll

17 — CONTROL SNAP
Violently resist losing control

18 — HYPER-FOCUS
Fixate on one actionable goal
GM defines task. +2 to related rolls. Cannot take unrelated actions.
19 — MOMENT OF CLARITY

04 // COMBAT REFERENCE
TURN STRUCTURE
  • Initiative: d10 + AWARENESS
  • Each Turn: 1 Action + 1 Minor Action
  • Attack: d10 + Attribute + Skill vs TN
  • Movement: 1 zone as Minor Action; 2 zones as Minor (–1 COMPOSURE) or full turn (no penalty)
  • Zones: CLOSE / NEAR / FAR / OUT OF REACH
REACTIONS (2 per operative / long rest)
  • Overwatch: If Aimed last turn — shoot enemy entering line of sight
  • Counterattack: If attacked in melee — immediate return strike
  • Protect: Take a hit for adjacent ally
  • Brace: Reduce incoming damage by 2
WEAPONS
  • Light (pistol, knife): 1d10
  • Standard (rifle, shotgun): 2d10
  • Heavy (sniper, LMG): 3d10 — cannot move and attack
ARMOR
  • Light: –2 damage, no penalty
  • Medium: –4 damage, –1 Mobility
  • Heavy: –6 damage, –1 Mobility, extended move costs +1 COMPOSURE
HEALTH
  • HP: 10 + BODY
  • COMPOSURE: 5 + RESOLVE
  • COMPOSURE = 1: –1 to BODY checks
  • COMPOSURE = 0: SPIKE Save vs TN 14, –1 to all rolls

FAST BUILD

Pick a role → Assign HP → Give 1 trait → Done in 10 seconds. When in doubt: attack roll + damage.

SPIKE PRESSURE ENEMIES

Some enemies should attack SPIKE instead of HP. On hit: +1 SPIKE. Aura: SPIKE Save each turn. Death effect: nearby PCs +1 SPIKE. Most enemies do not use COMPOSURE — reserve it for Awoken enemies, psychological threats, and bosses. COMPOSURE 5–10; at 0, enemy becomes erratic or escalates.

Play enemies thematically, not optimally. Keep turns under 10 seconds. Traits create identity. If you forget a rule, default to: attack roll + damage.

06 // MYSTERY BUILDER
CORE PRINCIPLE

The mystery is built to be solved — but only if the details are tracked. Information is currency. Every clue should raise a question before it answers one. Threads connect slowly. The picture that emerges may not be the expected one.

STEP 01
ESTABLISH THE TRUTH
Know what actually happened before you begin. Write it in one sentence. The players are not discovering your plot — they are uncovering reality. The difference matters.
STEP 02
LAYER CLUES IN THREES
Every key piece of information should be discoverable via at least three separate paths (objects, people, environments). If one is missed, the others remain. Never hide the only copy of critical information.
STEP 03
LET CLUES RAISE QUESTIONS FIRST
A clue that immediately explains something is information. A clue that creates more questions first — and then explains — is a discovery. Design for the second. The strangeness is information.
STEP 04
CONTROL THE INFORMATION RATE
One revelation per session maximum. Space revelations with accumulation — small, disconnected details that suddenly click. If players are connecting dots faster than expected: add complications, not more clues.
STEP 05
BUILD CONSEQUENCES, NOT OUTCOMES
Don't plan what the players will discover. Plan what exists and what it means. Let the players create the outcome through their choices. If they miss a clue: the world reacts. If they find one early: escalate.
STEP 06
MAKE COST VISIBLE IN PEOPLE
Show consequences in NPCs, behavior, and environments — not systems. A stable world should feel emotionally hollow. A harsh world should feel brutally alive but unjust. Exposition is a last resort.
CLUE TYPES (USE ALL THREE)
  • Physical: Objects, documents, physical evidence in the environment
  • Testimonial: What NPCs say, imply, contradict, or refuse to say
  • Behavioral: How the world acts, what is absent, what is wrong in ways that can't be spoken
WHEN PLAYERS ARE STUCK
  • Have an NPC notice what they missed
  • Let the environment change — escalate the situation
  • Introduce a complication that redirects, not resolves
  • Ask them: "What does your character feel is wrong here?" — let the SPIKE system carry it
07 // SESSION TOOLS
SESSION OPEN CHECKLIST
  • Recap last session — let players drive
  • Check in on open questions from last time
  • Confirm current SPIKE levels and HP
  • Identify any unresolved Emotional Flaws
  • Have one scene ready — don't plan the session, plan the situation
SESSION CLOSE CHECKLIST
  • Note what each player did — not what happened to them
  • Record any SPIKE changes, flaw triggers, and trait applications
  • Identify the next dangling thread to pull
  • Check in out-of-character if the session was heavy
  • Update party tracker if characters were modified
ADVANCEMENT — AFTER EACH MILESTONE

Players choose one of:

  • Gain 1 new Skill
  • Upgrade a Skill from +2 to +3 (Specialized)
  • Gain a Trait (max 6 per character, not stackable)
  • Increase max COMPOSURE by +1
  • Reduce a permanent Emotional Flaw (rare — requires meaningful narrative resolution)

Milestones are narrative beats, not experience points. A milestone is reached when the party understands something new about the world, makes a meaningful decision, or passes a point of no return.

EMOTIONAL FLAW IN PLAY

When a flaw triggers: "This hits your flaw — how do you react?"

  • Lean In: Act according to flaw → +1 to roll OR +1 COMPOSURE
  • Resist: d10+RESOLVE vs TN 11 → Fail: –1 roll OR +1 SPIKE

If a player consistently ignores a flaw: increase SPIKE or apply Disadvantage.

X-CARD PROTOCOL

Any player (including GM) may tap or hold up the X-card at any time. No explanation required. The scene shifts, fades, or cuts immediately.

Lines and Veils established in Session 0 are binding. The X-card overrides everything. This is always in effect.
